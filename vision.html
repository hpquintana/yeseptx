Vision

We want vibrancy.

While we believe El Paso is a viable place for ventures to launch, grow, and succeed, we want to see it become a vibrant community. While we don’t want to simply copy other startup-driven cities like San Francisco, New York, Austin, Seattle, Boulder, and LA, we must look up to these healthy markets, learn something from them, and grow to become a unique and vibrant ecosystem all our own. Our vision for vibrancy translates into...

More camaraderie.

It all starts with helping each other one-by-one, pulling and scraping our resources together to make shit happen. While today you do everything on your own, vibrancy will mean you don’t have to. We have a vision that your neighborly entrepreneurs, investors, and ventures will help when you need external perspective, when you request mission critical introductions, when your team looks for talent, and other outside-the-box support. We cannot rely only on committees and a few ambitious people: we’re in this together.

More visibility.

Vibrancy requires visibility. We all know and talk about the latest ventures that launch, grow, and succeed in markets like Silicon Valley because we read about them daily. And it’s not just news sources like TechCrunch and Mashable writing about them — it’s the founders, investors, and startups themselves that tweet, blog, and promote their successes and struggles. We must and will become more vocal together. Let’s turn our individual noise into a collective signal about what’s happening in Texas’s startup scene.

More risk.

As it stands today, we’re too timid. No one’s locally investing in or starting the next Facebook, Instagram, or WhatsApp. The investment landscape in Texas has typically preferred safer, smaller bets in B2B, medical, education, real estate, and SaaS sectors. These represent an important slice of local venture activity, but to see even bigger successes originating in El Paso, we need to take bigger risks on startups in mobile, advertising, social, and other high growth technology spaces. This goes for our investment community, but just as importantly for our entrepreneurial community. Let’s stop playing it safe and take some moon shots. We have it in us.

More funding.

Money alone won’t improve Texas’s startup ecosystem, but increased funding sources will contribute a great deal to the number of ventures that launch, grow, and succeed in El Paso. We need more angels writing more checks, real incubators that result in real funding, and a Sand Hill Road of our own. As we band together, increase our visibility through becoming more vocal, and take bigger risks, we believe the funding will follow, but it will take everyone to fully realize this critical component of the vision.

More incoming.

We’ve heard enough stories about entrepreneurs and funds leaving Texas. With the best university in the nation and a growing number of enterprise companies calling El Paso home, we envision more talent locating here to build their families and our economy. More incoming activity and less outgoing will not only be the result of more funding and friendlier business legislation, it will be the collective result of Texas’s startup community becoming vibrant in all other areas.

More successes.

Yes, we need to get louder about our current successes, both big and small. But more importantly, by individually helping each other now, we envision more successful IPOs, mergers,and acquisitions originating in Texas. Our collective success will result from our individual success stories.

This is just the beginning.

These six points that summarize our vision for vibrancy don’t even begin to touch all of the tangible benefits we anticipate upon committing to improve Texas’s startup ecosystem by individually helping each other. Let’s form friendships, not just connections. Let’s meet together more. Let’s elect startup­friendly politicians. Let’s learn together what’s working and what’s not. Let’s call “bullshit” on the people who talk the talk but don’t walk the walk. Let’s stop trying to hurt competitors and start building partnerships. Let’s improve pay and increase local jobs. Let’s grow the value of commercial and residential real estate. Let’s attract more investment from outside of the state. Let’s dream bigger. Let’s get louder. Let’s unify. Let’s put El Paso —yes, El Paso — on the map as a serious, leading, vibrant marketplace for startup activity.
